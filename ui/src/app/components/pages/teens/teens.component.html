Teen Instruction - 17 & Under

Teenagers (younger than 18) are required to take Behind The Wheel in order to obtain their license. This course consists of 7 sessions in the car in which the student will be driving and observing. At the end of the course the student will then take the a road skills test. Upon completion the student will receive a provisional driver's license.

The provisional driver's license along with the Learner’s Permit will be the license for the new driver and the student will be exempt from the road skills test at the DMV. Within the next few months the juvenile court will send a letter with the date and time of the Licensing Ceremony. At the Ceremony the student will receive the hard copy of the license.

    Requirements:
  • Driving Learner's Permit
  • Driver's Education Certificate. This can obtained from school's office. You can also take the course with us here.

This is an online class with the partnership of National Driver Training directed to students who have not taken the Driver Education Class in high school such as home schooled students or students moving from another country or state.

The program consists of at least 30 hours of instruction and includes components about alcohol safety, drug abuse awareness, aggressive driving, distracted driving, pedestrian and bicycle safety, handicapped parking, fuel efficient driving practices, motorcycle awareness and organ and tissue donation awareness as required by law.

You must be at least 15 years and 6 months old to take the online driver education course. All individuals who elect to take the classroom component of the driver education course online must take the online course through a DMV licensed driver training school. Homeschooled students are exempt from this requirement. In order to benefit from this course, you must be able to work independently and be comfortable with using a computer.

Student and Parent in Planning District 8 (Counties of Arlington, Fairfax, Loudoun, and Prince William and cities of Alexandria, Fairfax, Manassas and Manassas Park) must attend an additional minimum 90-minute parent/student driver education component as part of the in-classroom portion of the driver education curriculum emphasizing parental responsibilities regarding juvenile driver behavior, juvenile driving restrictions pursuant to Virginia Code and the dangers of driving while intoxicated and underage consumption of alcohol as required by the DMV.

If you are 18 and under the DMV requires 8 hour Driver’s Manual Course if you present proof that you have completed the 36 fifty minute periods of the Driver Education classroom component or 36 fifty-minute periods of the Driver Education if you have not previously taken the classroom component.
