Teen Instruction - 17 & Under

Teenagers (17 and under) are required to take Behind The Wheel in order to obtain their driver’s license. This course consists of 7 sessions in the car in which the student will be driving and observing. At the end of the course the student will then take a road skills test. Upon completion the student will receive a temporary driver's license. Within the next few months, the DMV will send the permanent license by mail.

    Requirements:
  • Driving Learner's Permit
  • Driver's Education Certificate. This can obtained from the high school's office. If you took it with other DMV Licensed school they will issue it. Otherwise you can take the class online with us.

This course is required by the DMV to obtain your license for students who have not taken the Driver’s Education class in high school, such as home schooled students and students coming from out of state.

The program consists of at least 30 hours of instruction and includes components about alcohol safety, drug abuse awareness, aggressive driving, distracted driving, pedestrian and bicycle safety, handicapped parking, fuel efficient driving practices, motorcycle awareness and organ and tissue donation awareness as required by law.

You must be at least 15 years and 6 months old to take the online driver education course. All individuals who elect to take the classroom component of the driver education course online must take the online course through a DMV licensed driver training school. Homeschooled students are exempt from this requirement.

In order to benefit from this course, you must be able to work independently and be comfortable with using a computer.

We offer this class online in affiliation with the National Driver Training Institute.

Post following procedures:
On the day of testing, you must bring the following to the test site:
  • Photo identification (DMV issued identification card, school issued identification card, valid passport or military identification).
  • Proof of completion of the on line driver education course.
  • A completed "Parental Consent for Online Driver Education Examination" (DTS 62), if you are under age 18.
  • Attend parent teen presentation or proof of completion of the additional 90-minute parent/teen course if you are under age 18 and live in Planning District 8 (counties of Arlington, Fairfax, Loudoun, and Prince William and cities of Alexandria, Fairfax, Manassas and Manassas Park).
  • The online driver training school will provide a computer for testing.

This course is either for people who have been mandated to take it (by the DMV or court) or for people who need to gain points on their driving record. It is designed to make you aware of rules and regulations of the road and become a safer driver.

Taking this course voluntarily will add 5 points to your driving record. We recommend you take this course voluntarily if you are facing a traffic violation in court. If you are 21 or younger you must attend this class in person.
