Adult Instruction - 18 & Over

Adults (18 and older) can take Behind the Wheel in order to obtain their driver’s license. This course consists of 7 sessions in the car in which the student will be driving and observing. At the end of the course the student will then take a road skills test. Once completed, the student will receive a "Completion Certificate - Adult Waiver" which can then be presented to the DMV to receive their driver's license. The student no longer will need to take the DMV road test or wait the holding period for the Learner's Permit.

    Requirements:
  • Driving Learner's Permit
  • Driver's Education Certificate. If this class was taken in high school, you can obtain the certificate from that high school's office. We also offer Driver's Education online for those who have not taken it, such as homeschooled students and students from out of state.

This course is required by the DMV to obtain your license for students who have not taken the Driver’s Education class in high school, such as home schooled students and students coming from out of state. The program consists of at least 30 hours of instruction and includes components about alcohol safety, drug abuse awareness, aggressive driving, distracted driving, pedestrian and bicycle safety, handicapped parking, fuel efficient driving practices, motorcycle awareness and organ and tissue donation awareness as required by law. In order to benefit from this course, you must be able to work independently and be comfortable with using a computer.

We offer this class online in affiliation with the National Driver Training Institute.

Post following procedures:
On the day of testing, you must bring the following to the test site:
  • Photo identification (DMV issued identification card, school issued identification card, valid passport or military identification).
  • Proof of completion of the online driver education course.
  • The online driver training school will provide a computer for testing.
Failed 3 times Learner's Permit Test:

If you have failed the Learner's Permit test 3 times this course is required by the DMV. The course consists of one 8 hour in person session where we will go over the information provided in the Virginia Driver's Manual.

Failed 3 times DMV Road Test:

If you have failed the DMV road test 3 times this course is required by the DMV. This course consists of 7 fifty-minute classes in our student instruction vehicle. When completed, we will use a certificate that allows the student to take the road test at the DMV again.
